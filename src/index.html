Industrial control systems connected to internet

In 2019 there are approximately 3500 industrial control systems connected to Internet. These range from individual computers controlling machines directly to systems controlling and monitoring bigger geographical areas.

Triton/Trisus in the oil refinery

Machines in factories around the world are nowadays controlled and monitored by computer systems known as industrial control systems these are often directly or indirectly connected to Internet. This makes the operation of a factory more convenient and efficient but at the same poses a considerable risk for the security of the factory.

In June 2017 an undisclosed oil refinery in Saudi Arabia shut down. At first the accident seemed to be caused by a malfunctioning equipment. A month later the refinery shut down again.

  • The unaware control & monitoring system

    The system responsible for monitoring and control (known as Distributed Control System) of the machine doesn't report any problems.

  • August 2017 The group triggers the malware again

  • Gaining access to the safety systems

    The group uses remote access trojan or RAT malware to gain control over the laptop and devices connected to it. To hide itself the RAT pretends to be genuine Triconex application.

  • Triconnex Safety Instrumented System

    The laptop is used to program one of the Triconex safety systems responsible for monitoring and shutting down a production machine or the refinery if the machine's operation should become dangerous. The group can command the machine or write new programs into its firmware.

  • Affecting a production machine

    The hackers can use the infected SIS to shutdown the production machine or reprogram it to allow unsafe state. In the worst case scenario this could lead to release of toxic hydrogen sulphide gases or explosion of high pressure, high temperature materials.

  • A bug in the malware

    An alleged bug in the malware caused the safety system to enter the fail safe state and shutdown the refinery

  • No suspicions raised

    The shutdown is ascribed to the malfunctioning safety system rather than possibility of insider or outsider attack

  • 6 infected laptops & safety systems

    One of the safety machines enters fail safe state and shuts the refinery down again

  • Suspicions are raised and cyber security firms are called to investigate and clean up
